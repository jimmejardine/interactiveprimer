Probability Basics

Probability measures how likely something is to happen. Every probability is a number from 0 to 1:

  • 0 means the event is impossible — it never happens;
  • 1 means it is certain — it always happens;
  • 0.5 means an even chance — just as likely to happen as not.

The same probability can be written as a fraction, a decimal or a percentage — for example \tfrac{1}{2} = 0.5 = 50\%. We describe events in everyday words too, sliding from one end of the scale to the other:

\text{impossible} \;\to\; \text{unlikely} \;\to\; \text{even chance} \;\to\; \text{likely} \;\to\; \text{certain}
  • Every probability lies in the range 0 \le P \le 1.
  • P = 0 means the event is impossible.
  • P = 1 means the event is certain.
  • P = 0.5 is an even chance — halfway along the scale.
