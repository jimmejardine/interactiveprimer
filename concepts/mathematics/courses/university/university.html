Undergraduate Mathematics

A university mathematics degree is a three-year climb from the tools of school into the structures of modern mathematics — where every idea is stated precisely and proved. Year 1 rebuilds calculus, algebra and logic on rigorous foundations; Year 2 opens the core theories — analysis, groups and rings, complex functions; Year 3 fans out into the advanced landscape: measure theory, functional analysis, Galois theory, geometry and more. This is the shape of a typical honours degree, mapped onto the tree.

The three years

  • Year 1 — Foundations — proof, calculus, linear algebra, discrete maths and a first look at groups.
  • Year 2 — Core — real & complex analysis, rings & groups, numerical methods and optimization.
  • Year 3 — Advanced — measure theory, functional analysis, Galois theory, geometry and specialised topics.
