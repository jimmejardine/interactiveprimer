Probability & Inference

Most of mathematics asks "given the cause, what is the effect?" Inference runs the other way: given the noisy effects we observe, what can we believe about the cause? This short course builds the core machinery of statistical inference — the language of updating beliefs in the face of uncertain data. It is the Bayesian arc of the wider statistics course: where classical statistics asks how surprising the data would be, the Bayesian view asks how the data should update what we already believed.

It is the companion the Bayesian view of inverse problems depends on: data is always a forward model plus noise, and recovering the cause means weighing what the data says against what we knew beforehand.

The path

  1. Bayes' Theorem — the rule for updating a belief with evidence.
  2. Likelihood and MLE — how well a parameter explains the data, and picking the best one.
  3. The Covariance Matrix — how several uncertain quantities vary together.
  4. The Multivariate Gaussian — the bell curve in many dimensions.
  5. MAP Estimation — combining a prior with the likelihood to get the most probable answer.
