Mathematics

Mathematics is the science of patterns — and the closest thing we have to magic that actually works. It lives entirely in your head, needs no batteries, and gives answers you can be certain about. Best of all, once you prove something is true, it stays true forever: the rule Pythagoras spotted about triangles 2,500 years ago is still exactly right today, and always will be.

a^2 + b^2 = c^2

The toolkit everything else borrows

Here's the sneaky part: maths is the toolkit every other subject reaches for. Physics writes the laws of the universe as equations; computer science is, underneath it all, just maths you can run on a machine. Learn maths and you've quietly picked the lock on both — which is why it's the trunk this whole tree of knowledge grows from.

The six great branches

Mathematics fans out into six great branches, and everything on this tree grows from one of them:

  • Foundations & Logic — the rules of proof, and the nature of sets and infinity.
  • Numbers & Algebra — from counting all the way to the abstract structures of groups and fields.
  • Analysis — the mathematics of limits, change and the infinitely small: calculus and everything above it.
  • Geometry & Topology — the study of shape and space, both rigid and stretchy.
  • Probability & Statistics — careful reasoning about uncertainty and data.
  • Discrete & Applied Mathematics — counting, computing, optimising, and modelling change.

New here? Start in Numbers & Algebra with Arithmetic and follow the branches upward — each page names the ideas it builds on, so you're never lost.
