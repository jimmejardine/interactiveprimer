Repeating Patterns

A repeating pattern is built from a small group that repeats over and over. That group is called the core. Once you spot the core, you can carry the pattern on forever — and say what comes next without drawing every step.

In circle, square, circle, square, circle, square, … the core is circle, square. It just keeps repeating, so the next shape must be a circle, then a square, and so on.

Find the core, then carry on

Step through the pattern. Find the core, then repeat it to work out what comes next.

  • the core is the group of shapes (or numbers, or colours) that repeats;
  • to continue a pattern, repeat the core from where it left off;
  • a growing pattern changes by the same amount each step instead of repeating — like 2, 4, 6, 8, \dots (add 2 each time). That idea grows up into the term-to-term rule of a number sequence.
