# Mathematics — planned concept tree (ages 5 → 18)

A road-map of (nearly) every mathematical idea needed to take a learner from age **5** to age **18**,
as a nested tree. It is the master plan for which `concepts/mathematics/**` pages to build, and a
gap analysis against what exists today.

**How to read it**

- **Nesting (≤5 levels):** Strand → Topic → Sub-topic → Concept → atomic idea. A **leaf** ≈ one
  teachable page ("one small idea per page").
- **Order = prerequisite order.** Earlier items are prerequisites for later ones; this is also the
  rough 5→18 progression.
- **Age bands** (UK-flavoured, but "what I'd teach" rather than tied to one syllabus):
  *Early* 5–7 · *Primary* 7–11 · *Lower secondary* 11–14 · *GCSE* 14–16 · *A-level* 16–18.
- **`✓ built: <slug>`** marks a leaf that already exists as a page (path under
  `concepts/mathematics/`). Some built pages currently live under `calculus/functions/…` etc.; the
  marker shows their *real* location even where this plan would group them differently.
- Everything without a ✓ is **planned** (not yet built).

### Notes & next steps
- **Strongest coverage today:** early *Arithmetic* and the whole *Calculus* strand.
- **Biggest gaps to build:** Fractions/Decimals/Percentages (beyond the basics), Ratio & Proportion,
  Measurement & Mensuration, most of *Geometry*, *Statistics*, *Probability*, and the
  middle of *Algebra* (equations, sequences, quadratics, graphs).
- A few built pages sit under `calculus/functions/…` although they are really *Algebra/coordinate
  geometry* foundations (functions, the plane, linear functions, slope) — consider relocating them
  to an `algebra/` (or `functions/`) home when that strand is fleshed out.
- When promoting a leaf to a real page, follow `CLAUDE.md`: one idea per page, set `prerequisites`
  to the leaves directly above/feeding it, and let levels propagate.
