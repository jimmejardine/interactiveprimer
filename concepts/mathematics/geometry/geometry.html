Geometry

Geometry is the mathematics of shape, size and position — points and lines, angles and triangles, circles and solids, and the way they move and fit together. It is the oldest branch of mathematics, and the most visual: nearly every result is something you can see, and prove with a careful picture.

The engine of geometry is the theorem: a fact, true for every figure of its kind, that you justify once and then use forever. This course builds them up in order — each new theorem leaning on the ones before — and lets you practise each by chasing angles and lengths right on the diagram.

The shape of the journey

Stage by stage, from a single angle to circles, solids, transformations — and the tilings that break the rules.

  • Stage 1 — Lines & angles. The alphabet: angles on a line, around a point, and the angles parallel lines make.
  • Stage 2 — Triangles. The angle sum, the exterior angle, congruence, similarity and Pythagoras.
  • Stage 3 — Quadrilaterals & polygons. Four-sided shapes and the angle rules for any polygon.
  • Stage 4 — Circles. The circle theorems — angles at the centre, in a segment, and in a semicircle.
  • Stage 5 — Area, perimeter & volume. Measuring flat shapes and solids.
  • Stage 6 — Coordinate geometry. Shape meets algebra on the grid.
  • Stage 7 — Transformations & symmetry. Sliding, turning, flipping and scaling figures.
  • Stage 8 — Tilings & aperiodic order. Tessellations, Penrose tilings and the quasicrystals that broke the rules.
