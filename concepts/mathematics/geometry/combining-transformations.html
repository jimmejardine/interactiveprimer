Combining Transformations

You can apply transformations one after another: take a shape, transform it, then transform the image again. For example, reflect a shape and then translate the result.

Often the combined effect of two transformations can be described as a single transformation:

  • two reflections in parallel mirror lines together make a translation;
  • two reflections in intersecting mirror lines together make a rotation about the point where they cross.

Be careful: the order usually matters. Doing transformation A then B generally lands the shape somewhere different from B then A.

Combining transformations

  • You can apply transformations one after another to the image of the previous one.
  • A single equivalent transformation often describes the combined effect.
  • Two reflections in parallel lines → a translation.
  • Two reflections in intersecting lines → a rotation about their intersection.
  • The order generally changes the result.
