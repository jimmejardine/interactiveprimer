Types of Quadrilateral

The six quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is any shape with four straight sides. There are six you meet again and again, each pinned down by one defining property:

  • a square has four equal sides and four right angles;
  • a rectangle has four right angles, with opposite sides equal;
  • a rhombus has four equal sides (a "pushed-over" square);
  • a parallelogram has both pairs of opposite sides parallel;
  • a trapezium has exactly one pair of parallel sides;
  • a kite has two pairs of adjacent equal sides.

Equal sides are shown with matching tick marks, parallel sides with matching arrowheads, and a right angle with a small square.

How they fit together

These shapes are not six separate boxes — the special ones are particular cases of the more general ones.

  • every quadrilateral's interior angles sum to 360^\circ;
  • a square is a special rectangle (it just also has equal sides) and a special rhombus (it just also has right angles);
  • a rectangle and a rhombus are both special parallelograms — once opposite sides are equal, they are automatically parallel.
