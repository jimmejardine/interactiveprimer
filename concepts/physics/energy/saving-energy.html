Saving Energy

Energy is precious. Most of the energy in our homes costs money and uses up fuel — and burning fuel is not good for our planet. So one of the kindest, cleverest things you can do is simple: don't waste it.

Wasting energy means using it when you don't need to — a light glowing in an empty room, a heater roaring while a door hangs open, hot water gushing down the drain. Every one of those is energy trickling away for nothing.

Easy ways to save energy

  • Turn off the lights when you leave a room.
  • Close doors so the heat stays in.
  • Pop on a jumper instead of turning the heating up.
  • Walk or cycle for a short trip instead of taking the car.
  • Turn the tap off while you brush your teeth.

Here is a little room with three things left running. The tall bar on the right is the energy wasted meter. Turn each thing off when nobody needs it and watch the waste bar drop — switch everything off and it falls all the way to nothing.

Energy sneaks away even when you think everything is off. A telly or a games console left on standby — that little red glow — is still nibbling energy all night. A charger left plugged in with nothing on it does the same. If you're really finished with something, switch it off at the wall.

When you feel cold, the quickest thought is to turn the heating up — but that warms the whole house, walls and ceiling and all, just to warm you. A jumper traps a thin layer of warm air right next to your skin, so you get toasty while barely any energy is used. It's like heating a cup of tea instead of the whole kitchen.
