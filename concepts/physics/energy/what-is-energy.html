What Is Energy?

Energy is the ability to make something happen or change. If a thing moves, lights up, warms up or makes a noise, energy did it. Take the energy away and everything simply stops.

You can't hold energy in your hand like a marble, but you can always spot it by what it does. A ball only rolls if something gives it energy. A phone only lights up if its battery has energy stored inside. Even you can't run about until you have eaten.

Energy comes from a source

Energy has to come from somewhere. We call that somewhere an energy source:

  • Food gives energy to you and every animal.
  • Fuel like petrol gives energy to a car.
  • A battery gives energy to a torch or a toy.
  • The Sun gives energy to plants so they can grow.

The more energy a source gives, the more it can make happen.

Let's fill a toy car's tank. Slide the energy in the tank up and down: the more energy you put in, the fuller the tank glows — and the further the car can travel. With an empty tank it can barely move at all.
