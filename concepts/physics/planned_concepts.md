# Physics — planned concept tree (ages 5 → 18)

A road-map of (nearly) every physics idea needed to take a learner from age **5** to age **18**,
as a nested tree. It is the master plan for which `concepts/physics/**` pages to build.

**How to read it**

- **Nesting (≤5 levels):** Strand → Topic → Sub-topic → Concept → atomic idea. A **leaf** ≈ one
  teachable page ("one small idea per page").
- **Order = prerequisite order**, which is also the rough 5→18 progression.
- **Age bands** (UK-flavoured, "what I'd teach"): *Early* 5–7 · *Primary* 7–11 ·
  *Lower secondary* 11–14 · *GCSE* 14–16 · *A-level* 16–18.
- **The primary strands are built; secondary is still planned.** When a leaf becomes a page, mark it
  `✓ built: <slug>` (path under `concepts/physics/`), as in the maths plan. See **Built so far** at the
  bottom for the current list. There is also a **UK physics course tree** under
  `concepts/physics/courses/` (Reception–Year 6 and GCSE Year 10–11) that curates these pages.
- Many leaves **depend on maths** (graphs, ratio, algebra, trig, calculus); those prerequisites
  live in `concepts/mathematics/`.

## 1. Working scientifically (the practical thread, 5–18)

### Early–Primary (5–11)
- Observing & questioning
  - Noticing and describing what happens
  - Asking testable questions; predicting
  - Sorting and classifying
- Fair tests
  - Variables: what to change, measure and keep the same
  - Repeating measurements
- Measuring
  - Measuring length, mass, time, temperature
  - Reading scales; choosing the right instrument
  - Units and why we standardise them
- Recording & presenting
  - Tables of results
  - Bar charts and line graphs; reading data

### Lower secondary–A-level (11–18)
- Quantities & units
  - SI base units and derived units
  - Prefixes (kilo, milli, micro …) and standard form
  - Scalars vs vectors
- Measurement & uncertainty
  - Precision, accuracy and resolution
  - Estimating uncertainty; significant figures
  - Combining/propagating uncertainties (A-level)
- Graphs & analysis
  - Plotting and interpreting graphs
  - Gradient and area-under-graph meanings
  - Linearising relationships; lines of best fit
- Experimental design & error
  - Systematic vs random error
  - Anomalies; reliability and validity

## 2. Forces & Motion (mechanics)

### Early–Primary (5–11)
- Pushes and pulls
  - Forces as pushes and pulls
  - Forces change speed, direction and shape
  - Friction (grip and slowing down)
  - Air and water resistance
  - Gravity pulls things down
  - Magnets push and pull (attract/repel)
- Floating, sinking and simple machines
  - Why things float or sink (intro)
  - Levers, gears and pulleys (everyday)

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Describing motion
  - Speed; distance–time graphs
  - Velocity and acceleration
  - Velocity–time graphs (gradient = acceleration, area = distance)
- Forces
  - Contact and non-contact forces; force diagrams
  - Resultant force; balanced vs unbalanced
  - Weight, mass and gravitational field strength
  - Newton's first law (inertia)
  - Newton's second law (F = ma)
  - Newton's third law (action–reaction)
  - Terminal velocity
- Force effects
  - Hooke's law (springs) and elastic energy
  - Moments, levers and gears
  - Pressure in fluids; upthrust

### A-level (16–18)
- Kinematics
  - Equations of motion (suvat)
  - Projectile motion
  - Motion graphs from calculus (v = ds/dt, a = dv/dt)
- Dynamics
  - Free-body diagrams; resolving forces
  - Friction and inclined planes
  - Momentum and impulse; conservation of momentum
  - Collisions (elastic/inelastic)
- Rotational & periodic motion
  - Circular motion (centripetal force)
  - Simple harmonic motion
  - Gravitation: Newton's law of gravitation; orbits

## 3. Energy

### Primary (7–11)
- Everyday energy
  - Energy makes things happen; sources of energy
  - Light, heat, sound, movement, electrical energy (recognising)
  - Saving energy

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Energy stores & transfers
  - Energy stores (kinetic, gravitational, elastic, thermal, chemical, …)
  - Transfers by heating, work, electricity, radiation
  - Conservation of energy; Sankey diagrams
- Calculating energy
  - Work done = force × distance
  - Kinetic energy
  - Gravitational potential energy
  - Power = energy ÷ time
  - Efficiency
- Thermal energy
  - Conduction, convection and radiation
  - Specific heat capacity
  - Reducing unwanted transfers (insulation)
- Energy resources
  - Renewable vs non-renewable
  - National and global energy use

### A-level (16–18)
- Work, energy & power (vector treatment)
- Elastic potential energy and the work–energy theorem

---

## 4. Electricity & Magnetism

### Primary (7–11)
- Simple circuits
  - Making a bulb light; complete circuits
  - Switches; conductors and insulators
  - Changing brightness/loudness in a circuit

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Charge & current
  - Static electricity; charging by friction
  - Electric charge and current
  - Potential difference (voltage)
  - Resistance; Ohm's law
- Circuits
  - Series circuits
  - Parallel circuits
  - I–V characteristics (resistor, filament lamp, diode)
  - Resistors, thermistors and LDRs
  - Electrical power and energy; cost of electricity
- Mains electricity
  - a.c. vs d.c.; the three-pin plug and safety
- Magnetism & electromagnetism
  - Magnets and magnetic fields
  - The magnetic field of a current; electromagnets
  - The motor effect; the d.c. motor
  - Electromagnetic induction; generators and transformers (intro)

### A-level (16–18)
- Circuits (deeper)
  - Kirchhoff's laws; internal resistance and EMF
  - Potential dividers
- Fields
  - Electric fields (Coulomb's law, field & potential)
  - Magnetic fields and forces on currents/charges
  - Electromagnetic induction (Faraday's & Lenz's laws)
- Capacitance (charge, energy, RC circuits)

## 5. Waves

### Primary (7–11)
- Light & sound (everyday)
  - Light travels; we see when light enters the eye
  - Shadows; reflections in mirrors
  - Sound is made by vibrations; pitch and volume
  - Sound travels through materials

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Wave basics
  - Transverse and longitudinal waves
  - Amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period
  - Wave speed (v = fλ)
- Light & optics
  - Reflection (law of reflection)
  - Refraction
  - Lenses and image formation; the eye and the camera
  - Colour and filters
- Sound
  - How sound travels; the speed of sound
  - Echoes and ultrasound
  - Hearing and the ear
- The electromagnetic spectrum
  - The EM spectrum and its uses
  - Dangers of different EM radiations

### A-level (16–18)
- Wave behaviour
  - Superposition and interference
  - Standing/stationary waves
  - Diffraction; the diffraction grating
  - Polarisation
- Optics & light
  - Total internal reflection; optical fibres
  - Lenses (deeper)
- Quantum nature of light
  - The photoelectric effect; photons
  - Wave–particle duality; de Broglie wavelength

## 6. Matter & the particle model

### Primary (7–11)
- Materials & states
  - Solids, liquids and gases (properties)
  - Melting, freezing, evaporating, condensing
  - The water cycle
  - Reversible and irreversible changes

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Particle model
  - Particles in solids, liquids and gases
  - Density (mass ÷ volume)
  - Changes of state and the particle model
  - Internal energy; specific & latent heat
- Pressure
  - Pressure in gases; particle explanation
  - Pressure in liquids (depth); atmospheric pressure
  - Gas laws (pressure, volume, temperature)

### A-level (16–18)
- Thermal physics
  - Absolute (kelvin) temperature
  - The ideal gas equation
  - Kinetic theory of gases
  - First law of thermodynamics (intro)

## 7. Earth & Space

### Primary (7–11)
- Day, night and the year
  - Day and night; the spinning Earth
  - The Sun's apparent movement; shadows through the day
  - Seasons
  - The Moon and its phases
- The Solar System
  - The planets and the Sun
  - Earth, Sun and Moon as spheres

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Gravity & orbits
  - Gravity and weight on different bodies
  - Orbits of moons, planets and satellites
- Stars & the universe
  - The life cycle of a star
  - Galaxies and the scale of the universe
  - Red-shift and the expanding universe; the Big Bang

### A-level (16–18)
- Astrophysics (option)
  - Stellar properties; the Hertzsprung–Russell diagram
  - Cosmology and the fate of the universe

---

## 8. Atomic & Nuclear Physics

### Lower secondary–GCSE (11–16)
- Atomic structure
  - The atom: protons, neutrons, electrons
  - Atomic number, mass number, isotopes
  - Development of the atomic model (plum-pudding → nuclear)
- Radioactivity
  - Alpha, beta and gamma radiation
  - Nuclear equations
  - Half-life and activity
  - Uses and dangers of radiation; background radiation
- Nuclear energy
  - Nuclear fission
  - Nuclear fusion (in stars and reactors)

### A-level (16–18)
- Particle physics
  - Fundamental particles; quarks and leptons
  - Antimatter; the standard model (intro)
  - Mass–energy equivalence (E = mc²)
- Nuclear physics (deeper)
  - Binding energy and stability
  - Radioactive decay law (calculus link)
