Levers, Gears and Pulleys

Could you lift a grown-up off the ground with one hand? On a playground see-saw, you can! A see-saw is a kind of simple machine — a clever tool that lets a small push do a big job. Simple machines don't make new force out of nowhere; they just spread your effort out cleverly so a little goes a long way.

Three of the most useful ones are the lever, the pulley and the gear.

A lever is a stiff bar that tips on a balancing point called the pivot (a see-saw's is the bar in the middle). The trick is where you push. Push far from the pivot and your push is magnified into a big lift near the pivot on the other side. Push right next to the pivot and you have almost no power at all.

Try the see-saw. A heavy block sits on the left, near the pivot. Slide the arrow to choose where you push down on the right. Push close to the middle and the block is too heavy to shift. Move your push right out to the far end and — up it goes! The same push does far more work from further out.

Pulleys and gears

  • A pulley is a wheel with a rope over it. Pulling down on the rope lifts a load up — and it is easier to pull down (you can use your own weight) than to heave something straight up. Flags and window blinds work this way.
  • Gears are wheels with teeth that lock together. As one turns, its teeth push the next one round, passing the turning along. Bikes, clocks and hand-whisks are full of them.

A simple machine does not give you free force. On a lever, when you push far out to lift the load easily, your end of the bar has to travel a long way while the load only rises a little. You swap a big, easy movement for a small, powerful one — you never get something for nothing.

A very old scientist named Archimedes once boasted, "Give me a place to stand and a long enough lever, and I could move the Earth." He was right in theory — with a bar long enough and a pivot to rest it on, even a tiny push could lift our whole planet. The catch? Your end of the lever would have to swing across billions of miles of space to raise the Earth by the tiniest hair. A wonderful idea, but you would be pushing for rather a long time.
